Science researches say that the global temperature has risen by 0.9 degrees Celsius since the late 19th century and will continue to rise for decades.

Scientists say that if all land ice melted, sea level would rise approximately 70 meters worldwide. In fact, several ice caps, glaciers and ice shelves have already melted during this century.

Rain forest destruction plays its part on climate change issue as well. That is because trees store carbon dioxide as they grow.

Since the middle of 18th century, the acidity of surface ocean waters has increased by about 30 percent. This increase is the result of humans emitting more carbon dioxide into the atmosphere and as a result more of it is being absorbed into the oceans.

Scientific evidence shows that human-caused climate change is increasing extreme events happening all over the world such as: heat waves, coastal flooding, extreme precipitation events and severe droughts.
